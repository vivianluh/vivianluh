This browser-based interactive project showcases a small, cartooned animation that is prompted as soon as the user clicks and drags their cursor around the page. The animation will move forwards or backwards in relation to the position of the user's cursor and will stop on its frame as soon as the user's mouse button is lifted. The animation consists of a person who seems rather dull and emotionless, however there is a certain hidden frame which reveals their 'fun' side. Users are encouraged to play around with the interactive animation in the hopes that when they lift their mouse up, the animation pauses on that hidden frame.

The adjective and principle pairing this interactive animation reflects is Playfulness and Feedback and Agency. I wanted to explore the relationship and the reaction the user has when a digital environment returns something back (whether that be a signal, movement, a change in visuals) as a result of their actions. The reaction I want the user to radiate is one of playfulness and glee which will only be achieved if the digital environment is clear in its interactive instructions and the certain 'change' is one that has a playful and childlike aesthetic. Through this, I have decided to build an interactive animation that starts and changes the direction of an animation in accordance with the user's positioning of their mouse. This gives them the power to find and pause on that 'fun' hidden frame in the hopes that they find pleasure in an inherently amusing animation, forming a playful way of exerting the most out of the animation and the user's agency. With this interaction, hopefully the animation visuals and emotive goals are presented in the best light. The utilisation of a bold and bright colour palette and the simple cartooned artistic style aims to strengthen the playful aesthetic that is frequently seen in a variety of different cartoons and is directly inspired by the cartooning seen by Matt Groening. Moreover, the 'Information' button at the top left of the browser and the written instructions are also animated to push for this aesthetic. This art piece explores a variety of technical implementations to create a fully-fledged moving animation, such as using arrays and setInterval to establish movement. In order to enact the moving frames, I referenced this tutorial which went through setting up a setInterval function that moved the frames forwards and backwards. Instead of trying to track a video utilising the user's mouse, the implementation of frames was a much more orderly process. To build user interactivity, I wanted the user's mouse/trackpad to be the main output for this as it is one of the most used key amongst digital environments. When facilitating this, I used a series of event listeners to call for a mousedown, mousemove and mouseup event to authorise a Boolean variable for the user's position to then reference the setInterval functions I had already established. These technical design decisions were the most efficient in planning out and enacting the interactive elements and in creating a workflow that was succinct and ordered.
